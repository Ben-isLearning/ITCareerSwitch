Margin: / Between Border and other Elements

The Margin Property takes 4, 3, 2 or 1 Value.
Margin: 5px 10px 15px 5px; Sets top, right, bottom, left (like a compass)
Margin: 5% auto; Sets Vertical to 5%, and Horizontal to Auto
Margin: 1em auto 2em; Sets top to 1 em, horizontal to auto, and bottom to 2em
Margin: 2em; Sets all 5 sides the same
Margin: Is shorthand for Margin-left: Margin-right: Margin-top: Margin-bottom:


Border: / Border of Content

The Border Property takes 3, Values. Thickness, Style, Color
Border: 5px dotted blue; Sets overall border
Border: Is shorthand for border-left: border-right: border-top: border-bottom:
Thickness: px
Styles are: None, Inset, Outset, Double, Groove, Ridge, Solid, Dashed, Dotted.
Color: Pick one
border-radius: (1 - 4 values) TL / TR / BR / BL


Padding: / Between Content and Border

The Margin Property takes 4, 3, 2 or 1 Value.

Padding: 1em auto 2em; Sets top to 1 em, horizontal to auto, and bottom to 2em

The Cascade

Is the Order of precedence;
Browser Defaults > External Stylesheets > Embedded Styles > Inline Styles
Browser defaults have the least precedence
External Stylesheets override Browser Defaults
Embedded Styles override both Browser Defaults and External Style Sheets
Inline styles have the most precedence, they override everything
Whatever style rule has the most precedence, will be applied to the webpage
